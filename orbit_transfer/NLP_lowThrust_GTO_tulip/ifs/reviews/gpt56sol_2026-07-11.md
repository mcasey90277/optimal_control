## GPT-5.6-sol response

## 1. Formulation

The hard-throttle, explicit-switch-node multiple-shooting formulation is standard and sound. With free switch times, continuity plus \(S(\tau_i)=0\) supplies the usual corner condition: Hamiltonian continuity follows because the Hamiltonian jump is proportional to \(S\,\Delta u\). Saltation matrices are unnecessary—they are the equivalent sensitivity machinery when switches are detected implicitly as events.

One major qualification: fixing both \(\tau_f\) and \(t(\tau_f)=t_f\) generally adds the unintended constraint
\[
\tau_f=\int_0^{t_f}\kappa(x(t))^{-1}\,dt.
\]
Unless Sundman length is genuinely prescribed, use free \(\tau_f\), or normalize \(\sigma\in[0,1]\) and introduce an unknown scale:
\[
dt/d\sigma=\tau_f\kappa,\qquad dx/d\sigma=\tau_f\kappa f.
\]
The corresponding artificial-final-time transversality must then be included. Otherwise the certificate applies to the fixed-\(t_f\), fixed-Sundman-length problem, not strictly the original problem.

Also verify that \(\lambda_m(t_f)=0\) and the constant “1” in \(S\) come from one consistent fuel objective representation and normal multiplier normalization.

## 2. Stall diagnosis

A vanishing short arc is a very credible cause. As its duration tends to zero:

- the two switch conditions coalesce;
- propagation across the intervening arc approaches identity;
- perturbing its entry and exit times produces nearly cancelling effects;
- the two explicit node states become redundant.

That is a standard structural rank loss at a switch-pair birth/death. I would call it a **vanishing bang/coast arc near a switching-structure fold**, not yet Fuller chattering. Fuller behavior requires a sequence of accumulating switches associated with a singular/high-order structure; one close pair is insufficient evidence.

Two cautions:

1. Two steep simple crossings are not literally a double root yet. The limiting fold has \(S=\dot S=0\); your current pair may simply lie near that bifurcation.
2. For a square numerically nonsingular \(J\), \(R\in\operatorname{range}(J)\) is nearly automatic. Moreover, \(\operatorname{cond}(J)\) is scaling-dependent. Confirm the diagnosis from the smallest singular vectors of a physically scaled Jacobian and show they localize to the pair’s center/width and node variables.

Your sigmoid stick-breaking likely worsens the crawl: when the desired arc duration approaches zero, its derivative with respect to the unconstrained parameter also approaches zero. Thus LM sees both intrinsic structural degeneracy and a degenerating coordinate map.

## 3. Recommended sequence

1. **Audit the Sundman endpoint equivalence first.**
2. Replace the pair’s times temporarily by center/width variables, inspect scaled SVD vectors, and vary/freeze the width to verify \(\sigma_{\min}(J)\to0\).
3. **Do (A): remove both switches delimiting the tiny arc**, then re-solve. This remains a solution of the same original control problem only if the resulting trajectory satisfies the correct strict sign of \(S\) throughout the merged arc.
4. Apply canonical variable/residual scaling before judging conditioning: state scales, reciprocal costate scales, time/duration scales, continuity rows, terminal rows, and switching rows.
5. Continue in \(t_f\), adding or deleting switch pairs when a fold condition \(S=\dot S=0\) is encountered. Near folds, pseudo-arclength continuation is preferable to plain parameter stepping.
6. Use (B) as a clean benchmark afterward. A 50-switch Jacobian need not cost \(O(k^2)\): each arc depends only on its local initial node, duration/endpoints, and parameters. Local complex-step blocks or integrated variational equations/STMs give essentially \(O(k)\) propagation work.
7. Add interior non-switch nodes only where long-arc state-transition matrices are themselves ill-conditioned. They will not cure the vanishing-arc rank loss.

Changing LM to trust-region-reflective may help operationally, but it will not remove the structural singularity. Rank-revealing QR/SVD Newton steps and sensible scaling are more informative.

## 4. Main red flags

- Potential overconstraint from fixed \(\tau_f\) plus fixed physical \(t_f\).
- Sigmoid parameterization cannot represent the zero-duration boundary and becomes singular there.
- \(S=0\) at nodes is insufficient certification: verify the required sign over every complete arc and exclude singular intervals.
- Check Hamiltonian/costate transformation terms caused by \(\nabla\kappa\), and the constant \(\lambda_t\)/extended-Hamiltonian relation.
- Do not infer Fuller chattering from one disappearing switch pair.

Standard references: Bryson & Ho, *Applied Optimal Control* (1975); Ascher, Mattheij & Russell, *Numerical Solution of Boundary Value Problems* (1995) for multiple shooting; Maurer, Büskens, Kim & Kaya, “Optimization Methods for the Verification of Second-Order Sufficient Conditions for Bang-Bang Controls,” *OCAM* 26 (2005), 129–156; Fuller’s classical chattering example; and Betts, *Practical Methods for Optimal Control* (2010) for scaling and transcription conditioning.
