- **[METHODOLOGY]** `GTO_tulip/direct/sundman_minfuel/casadi_minfuel_sundman.m:222`, `solve_minfuel.m:12-13` — the flagship uses 4000 nodes over “~40 rev” (about **100 nodes/rev**), but no mesh family is run or stored. The reported `25` is a node-threshold count treated as exact. Reproduce at ≥3 meshes, report a switch-count band and ΔV/mass convergence, and store mesh metadata with every result.
- **[CORRECTNESS]** `sundman_homotopy.m:47-52,61-72`; `minfuel_at_tf.m:84-97,114-124` — a run is “certified” and saved if *any* homotopy rung is clean, even if the requested final `eps=0` rung failed. This can publish an ε>0 solution as min-fuel. Require success of the requested endpoint; otherwise save/report `achievedEps` explicitly and do not label it min-fuel.
- **[ROBUSTNESS]** `casadi_minfuel_sundman.m:138-143,176-190`; `minfuel_at_tf.m:84-89`; `gen_energy_seed.m:137-152` — continuation acceptance gates only solver exception status plus dynamics defect. IPOPT’s configured `acceptable_tol=1e-5` can accept weak stationarity despite `maxDefect<1e-6`; failed rungs are skipped rather than adaptively retried. Gate on `Solve_Succeeded`, primal/dual infeasibility, BC/unit residuals, and endpoint ε; halve continuation steps and raise `maxIter` before advancing.
- **[ROBUSTNESS]** `casadi_minfuel_sundman.m:114-119` — fixed velocity bounds `[-12,12]` are close to the stated GTO-perigee scale, and position/mass bounds are likewise silent active constraints. There is no bound-activity report, so a ladder result can be machine-feasible but box-limited. Widen physically nonessential state boxes, add primary-exclusion constraints, and reject/warn on near-active nonphysical bounds.
- **[CORRECTNESS]** `GTO_tulip/direct/PSR/psr_ipopt_certify.m:76-91` vs. `casadi_minfuel_sundman.m:219-226` — PSR expects `out.regHistory`, but the supplied solver never records it; the fallback then accesses absent `o.regHistory`. PSR stage 5b therefore cannot reliably complete. Capture and validate IPOPT iteration regularization history in the solver, or make the certifier return an explicit unavailable verdict without field access.
- **[ROBUSTNESS]** `minfuel_config.m:35-46`; `minfuel_at_tf.m:44-47`; `gen_energy_seed.m:62-64`; `gen_tulip_mintime.m:64-66` — thrust is campaign-global and hard-coded through `cfg`; min-time’s `cBox=[0.10,8]` and `tfCapMult=4` are fixed. A low/high-thrust ladder is not parameterized end-to-end and can become structurally box/cap-limited. Make thrust a rung parameter with provenance, recompute each rung’s min-time anchor, and adapt/diagnose `cScale`, time, and state bounds.

**Ladder-readiness:** It breaks first at configuration/anchoring: the nominal 25 mN values are injected by the drivers, while factors remain tied to nominal `tfMin`. Before starting: (1) parameterize and stamp thrust per rung, with a fresh min-time anchor; (2) replace fixed continuation/state/`cScale` boxes by adaptive, activity-checked bounds; (3) enforce endpoint-homotopy and full-KKT acceptance with retry subdivision. Add the mesh-convergence study before making switch-count claims.

**Overall verdict:** I trust the scoped 4000-node solution as a feasible, high-quality discrete result and its reported ΔV/defect, but not the exact “25 switches” publication claim without a mesh band. The highest-value improvement is an automated multi-mesh convergence/certification harness that records ΔV, mass, switch bands, active bounds, and endpoint-KKT status.
