## B. The five-layer plan

"Truly optimal" is five stacked claims, each with its own method and cost:

| # | Claim | Method | Status / effort |
|---|-------|--------|-----------------|
| 0 | Discrete KKT point of the NLP | IPOPT + defect check | **DONE** (2.4e-14) |
| 1 | First-order PMP extremal | duals → primer + transversality + switching sign law | ~90% done; ~1 h to close (§D) |
| 2 | Discretization-independent | no-resample mesh refinement; optional Hermite–Simpson cross-check | NOT DONE; ~half day |
| 3 | Regular switches / no singular arcs | margins of calibrated S(t) | NOT DONE; free by-product of 1 |
| 4 | Best among local minima (global *evidence*) | cross-seeded + multi-start t_f front, monotonicity test | NOT DONE; overnight compute |
| 5 | Independent indirect reproduction | Sundman multiple-shooting PMP solve seeded from duals | OPTIONAL (paper-grade); days |

### Layer 1 — close the first-order certificate (do this first)
Wire the §D prototype into `certify_minfuel_pmp.m`, replacing its failed
continuous-recovery front-end (§F) with a consumer of `out.lamDef`:
compute S(t), report burn/coast sign agreement with a ±1-node deadband at
switches, per-switch scale spread, λ_t constancy (§C check 3), and emit the
money figure — S(t) and throttle s(t) on twin axes with switch markers.
PASS thresholds (met by the prototype): sign agreement ≥99%, β spread ≤5%,
primerAlignDeg ≤0.2°, |lamMassEnd| ≤1e-3.

### Layer 2 — mesh-independence (the most under-verified claim)
The 2.4e-14 defect certifies the DISCRETE problem only; the min-time twin
already showed coarse meshes exploit discretization error. Refine WITHOUT
resampling (resampling is a known killer, §F): split every σ-interval in two,
warm-start states/controls by evaluating the converged solution at its own
midpoints (linear/pchip on the solution itself, not on a new mesh), re-solve at
ε=0 with `warmTight=true`. Accept when propellant changes <1e-4 kg and no
switch appears/disappears/moves by more than a node. Optional order
cross-check: one Hermite–Simpson re-solve (constraint machinery exists in
`../nlp_constraints_minfuel_hs.m` but is wired to fmincon — port through CasADi
or skip; fmincon stalls on this problem, §F).

### Layer 3 — regularity (nearly free)
From the calibrated S(t): (a) min |S| over each interior arc — must be bounded
away from 0 (no singular arcs; the min-time analog had S ∈ [−46.8, −2.5]);
(b) finite slope at each zero crossing (regular switches, no
Fuller/chattering). Only if a referee demands a true second-order sufficiency
test: re-parametrize by the ~25 switch times and check the reduced Hessian is
PD (Maurer/Osmolovskii style) — real work, low practical yield.

### Layer 4 — global evidence (the honest frontier)
PMP cannot certify globality, and the Jul-9 sweep PROVES dense local minima
(even the 1.15× anchor fell into a worse basin: 3.457 vs the certified 3.370).
Exploit a structural fact: the true min-fuel ΔV(t_f) front must be
**non-increasing in t_f** — any violation flags a non-global point.
(1) Cross-seed: re-solve each t_f warm-started from neighbors' solutions
(both directions; rescale via the no-resample seed map) and from the certified
point; keep the per-t_f ΔV-min; iterate until monotone.
(2) Multi-start per t_f with perturbed sharpening schedules; best-of-K.
(3) Report the claim honestly: "global within explored basins; 23% below the
best known competing (3-switch) local optimum; front-monotonicity consistent."

### Layer 5 — independent indirect reproduction (only for publication)
Sundman-regularized indirect MULTIPLE shooting (~20 arcs over the 40 revs;
per-arc STM conditioning ~10^(11/20) ≈ 3.5 — tame), seeded by β-scaled duals
from Layer 1, PMP residual → ~1e-10. Existing single-shooting min-fuel code
(`../../lowThrust_GTO_tulip/solve_minfuel_indirect.m` etc.) is the starting
point but MUST be Sundman-regularized and partitioned; plain single shooting
provably fails here (~1e6 sensitivity, §F).

## C. Data dictionary (exact layouts — verified against the code)

**`cr3bp_lt_params(0.025, 15, 2100)`** → struct p: `muStar` 0.012150585609624,
`lStar` 389703.264829278 km, `tStar` 382981.289129055 s, `c` (ND exhaust
velocity), `Tmax` (ND max accel at m=1), `g0`, `m0kg`, `Isp`.
ΔV = c·ln(1/mf)·lStar/tStar [km/s]; propellant = m0kg·(1−mf) [kg].

**Solver output struct `out`** (from `casadi_minfuel_sundman.m`):
- `X` [8×(N+1)]: rows 1:3 position r (ND, rotating frame), 4:6 velocity v,
  7 mass fraction m (m(1)=1), 8 carried physical time t (t(1)=0, t(end)=tf).
- `U` [4×(N+1)]: rows 1:3 unit thrust direction α (‖α‖=1 enforced), row 4
  throttle s ∈ [0,1]. Burn node ⇔ s>0.5.
- `lamDef` [8×N]: KKT multipliers of the dynamics-defect constraints — the
  DISCRETE COSTATES, one per interval k (between nodes k and k+1), rows
  [λ_r(3); λ_v(3); λ_m; λ_t]. Known only up to (i) a positive mesh-weight
  scale and (ii) a GLOBAL SIGN. Empirically the sign convention is such that
  the physical costate = **−lamDef** (i.e. use sign −1 in §D; equivalently
  −α'·(−λ_v)>0 on burns). Do not assume — verify via the primer.
- `lamAll`: full stacked lam_g. Constraint order = order of subject_to calls:
  defects 8N first (COLUMN-MAJOR: D(:), so interval-1 rows 1:8, interval-2
  rows 9:16, …), then N+1 unit-sphere equalities, then bounds-as-constraints
  (X≥lb, X≤ub, U≥lb, U≤ub as inequality multipliers), then boundary equalities.
  `lamDef = reshape(lamAll(1:8*N),8,N)` relies on this ordering — if you edit
  the solver's constraint order, this breaks silently.
- `primerAlignDeg`: mean angle (deg) between interval-midpoint thrust
  direction and −λ_v/‖λ_v‖ over burn intervals, global sign absorbed.
- `lamMassEnd` = lamDef(7,end): terminal mass costate (transversality ≈ 0).
- `maxDefect`, `switches` (count of s crossing 0.5), `edge` (fraction of nodes
  with s>0.95 or s<0.05), `mf`, `success`, `ipoptStatus`.

**`sundman_minfuel_certified.mat`**: variables `out` (as above, but SAVED
BEFORE dual extraction existed — `lamDef` may be absent; regenerate with one
warm-started ε=0 re-solve if needed), `sigma` [(N+1)×1 nodes], `rv0`, `rvf`,
`tauf0`, `pSund` (=1.5), `eps` (homotopy value at save), `tbl`.
N+1 = 4001 nodes.

**`tf_sweep_results.mat`**: `tfMin` = 6.290694; `results` struct array,
**variable length** — `run_tf_sweep.m` overwrites it and the entry count
changes (a full run was 7 factors 1.10…1.80; a partial/in-progress run may hold
1). ALWAYS `numel(results)`, never assume 7. Fields per entry:
`factor, tf_days, dV, prop_kg, switches, edge, defect, success, tf, X, U,
lamDef, primerAlignDeg, lamMassEnd`. These DO have duals — use them to test
anything without re-solving.

**Three cheap consistency checks on any `lamDef`:**
1. Primer: −λ_v/‖λ_v‖ ∥ α on burn intervals (expect ≤0.2° mean).
2. Transversality: |lamDef(7,end)| small (≤1e-3; best runs 1e-7).
3. λ_t near-constancy: row 8 SHOULD be roughly constant across intervals
   (autonomous dynamics ⇒ dλ_t/dτ = −∂H/∂t = 0). **Tested 2026-07-09 (f=1.15):
   it is only APPROXIMATELY constant — mean −0.47, std ≈ 12%, with outlier
   spikes (max deviation ~2.7).** So this is a SOFT sanity check, not a sharp
   bug detector: a real extraction bug at the ~10% level would hide in the
   existing scatter. Use it only to catch gross errors (wrong row, sign chaos),
   and if you need it sharper, first characterize where the spikes sit (likely
   switches/perigees) before trusting a tolerance.

## D. Validated switching-law prototype (copy-paste ready)

Validated 2026-07-08 on `tf_sweep_results.mat` (then 2 entries; re-run cheap).
The trick: S = 1 − β·W with ONE unknown scale β pinned by least squares on
S=0 at the switch intervals. This ABSORBS the trapezoid-weight/τ_f/κ covector
scaling that two external reviews disagreed about — no de-scaling needed.
The per-switch spread of β doubles as the empirical bound on the "fixed-τ_f
isoperimetric taint" concern (Gemini review): a tainted costate could not fit
all switches with one β to ~1%.

```matlab
% Switching-function sign law from discrete costates (route: empirical scale).
addpath('/Users/msc/Desktop/optimal_control/orbit_transfer/GTO_tulip/sundman_minfuel');
p = cr3bp_lt_params(0.025,15,2100);  c = p.c;
S = load('tf_sweep_results.mat');
for e = 1:numel(S.results)
    R = S.results(e);
    X = R.X;  U = R.U;  lamDef = R.lamDef;   % [8xnN],[4xnN],[8xN]
    nN = size(X,2);  N = nN-1;  s = U(4,:);
    mMid  = 0.5*(X(7,1:end-1) + X(7,2:end));         % interval-midpoint mass
    lamV  = lamDef(4:6,:);  nlamV = sqrt(sum(lamV.^2,1));
    lamM  = lamDef(7,:);
    burnI  = (s(1:end-1) > 0.5) & (s(2:end) > 0.5);  % strict burn intervals
    coastI = (s(1:end-1) < 0.5) & (s(2:end) < 0.5);
    swI = min(find(diff(double(s>0.5)) ~= 0), N);    % switch intervals
    sg = -1;                                          % global sign (verified)
    W  = nlamV.*c./mMid + sg*lamM;                   % S = 1 - beta*W
    beta = sum(W(swI))/sum(W(swI).^2);               % LS of 1 = beta*W at switches
    Sfun = 1 - beta*W;
    fprintf(['factor=%.2f: beta=%.4g spread=%.1f%% S<0|burn=%.1f%% ' ...
             'S>0|coast=%.1f%% lam_t drift=%.1e\n'], R.factor, beta, ...
        100*std(1./W(swI))/abs(mean(1./W(swI))), ...
        100*mean(Sfun(burnI)<0), 100*mean(Sfun(coastI)>0), ...
        max(abs(lamDef(8,:)-mean(lamDef(8,:)))));    % lam_t constancy check
end
```

**Expected output** (factor 1.15 RE-VERIFIED 2026-07-09 on the live file:
beta=0.0309, spread=1.1%, S<0|burn=99.7%, S>0|coast=99.7% — reproduces exactly;
factor 1.10 measured 2026-07-08):
```
factor 1.15: beta=0.0309  spread=1.1%   S<0|burn=99.7%  S>0|coast=99.7%
factor 1.10: beta=0.0307  spread=2.6%   S<0|burn=99.5%  S>0|coast=99.9%
```
With sign +1 instead of −1 the fit degrades unmistakably (spread 51–90%,
burn agreement 60–80%) — that contrast is itself the sign determination.
The disagreeing intervals hug the switches, but **not as tightly as ±1**:
at f=1.15 all 13 violators are within **3 intervals** of a switch. Use a
**±3-node deadband** (not ±1) when reporting a headline pass rate.
The λ_t-constancy line in the snippet prints an ABSOLUTE max-drift; interpret
it against std≈12% (see §C check 3), not as "should be ~0".

## F. Do-NOT-redo list (hard-won failures + resolved disputes)

1. **Continuous-costate recovery from the primal is a dead end** for the FULL
   certificate. Tried three ways in `certify_minfuel_pmp.m` (2026-07-08):
   forward-shoot λ(0) + fit (costate map amplifies ~5e11 over 40 revs);
   global min-norm LS (collapses λ_v; primer error pins at exactly 2.0);
   hard-constrained saddle/lsqlin with sign constraints (recursion residual
   reaches 3e-14 — conditioning SOLVED — but ~24% of burn directions remain
   unfittable: the hand-built trapezoidal adjoint is only a 2nd-order
   approximation of the continuous adjoint, and over 40 revs its error makes
   distant burn arcs mutually inconsistent; the bad arcs are at PERIGEE, not
   the coarse mesh). Lesson: certify a DISCRETE optimum with its OWN duals.
   Full autopsy in `TIER1_PMP_CERTIFICATION_SCOPE.md`; the file
   `certify_minfuel_pmp.m` still contains that failed front-end — replace it.
   The continuous recovery matched ~76% of directions, fine as a diagnostic,
   never a certificate.
2. **Single shooting over the full spiral fails, any objective** (~1e6
   sensitivity; ‖R‖ stalls ~0.2 even from covector seeds). Multiple shooting
   or nothing (Layer 5).
3. **Do not resample a 40-rev trajectory onto a new mesh** for warm starts —
   leaves an irreducible ~1e-2 defect that pins IPOPT in restoration. Always
   the no-resample discipline (`sundman_seed_map.m` pattern).
4. **Do not free τ_f as a decision variable** — dense KKT column → MUMPS
   fill-in → OOM/SIGKILL at N≳400. The fixed-τ_f "isoperimetric taint" concern
   (reviews/ Gemini) is real in principle but empirically bounded tiny here:
   primer 0.058° + single-β switch fit at 1% spread. Report those numbers as
   the answer; a toy study (`../../test_tauf.m` stub) is optional.
5. **fmincon (interior-point + lbfgs) plateaus at ~1e-3** on every min-fuel
   variant of this problem — don't go back; CasADi+IPOPT exact Hessian +
   Sundman is the working combination. `warmTight=true` for re-solving AT a
   bang-bang point; `warmTight=false` for genuine continuation moves — using
   the wrong one wedges IPOPT (inf_du blowups / false infeasibility).
6. **External reviews disagreed on the covector de-scaling** (whether ν_k maps
   to λ(t_k) with or without h·κ factors; `reviews/*2026-07-08*`). The §D
   empirical-β route makes the dispute moot for the sign law. If you later
   need ABSOLUTELY scaled costates (Layer 5 seeding), determine the scale by
   matching β, not from first principles, then verify by propagating one arc.
7. GPT-5.5's review cited line numbers for dual-extraction code that did not
   exist at the time (it was added later by the user) — treat that review's
   concepts as sound but its line references as stale.
