# Reading `results/plots/front_honest.png` — the honest ΔV vs t_f front

Audience: another AI (or a human collaborator) coming to this plot cold. This
document explains exactly what is plotted, what every color and line means, how
the certification tiers are computed, and what the plot does and does not claim.
Generated by `aggregate_front.m`; certification logic in `verify_tf_front.m`.
Written 2026-07-09.

---

## 1. The problem the plot summarizes

Minimum-fuel low-thrust transfer from GTO to a tulip orbit in the Earth–Moon
CR3BP. Spacecraft: 15 kg, 25 mN, Isp 2100 s (a ~40-revolution spiral). The
transfer time t_f is **fixed** in each optimization; we solve the min-fuel
problem independently at many values of t_f and plot the resulting ΔV. The
minimum possible transfer time is t_f_min = 6.2906939607 ND = 27.8845 days
(the min-time solution, which burns continuously and costs ΔV = 4.4665 km/s).
All t_f values are expressed as multiples of t_f_min ("factors": 1.12x, 1.65x,
etc.), though the plot's x-axis is in days.

Each solve is a Sundman-regularized trapezoidal collocation NLP (CasADi +
IPOPT), reached by a Bertrand–Épénoy energy-to-fuel homotopy. Every plotted
point has machine-tight dynamics defects (~1e-14) and exact boundary
conditions — **feasibility is never in question**. What varies between points
is how strongly we can certify *optimality*.

## 2. The core honesty problem the plot solves

A direct NLP solution is only a **local** minimum — and for this problem the
landscape has many local minima (solution families with different switch
counts, connected over a fold near 1.40–1.45x). Two failure modes a naive plot
would hide:

1. A converged point can be a *worse* local minimum than another solution we
   already have at the same t_f. Plotting only "the" solution per t_f would
   silently present dominated points as the front.
2. A converged point may not even satisfy the first-order Pontryagin
   necessary conditions when checked against its own KKT duals — i.e., the
   solver stopped somewhere that is feasible but not demonstrably extremal.

The honest-plot policy (see `HONEST_EVALUATION_DV_TF_FRONT.md`): **plot every
stored solution, classify each by how much verification it survives, and draw
envelopes that distinguish "certified" from "best known."**

## 3. Axes and the black square

- **x-axis:** transfer time t_f in days (range covers ~1.12x to 1.85x of
  t_f_min, i.e. roughly 31 to 52 days).
- **y-axis:** ΔV in km/s (computed from propellant used, ΔV = c·ln(m0/mf)).
- **Black filled square** at (27.88 d, 4.4665 km/s), labeled "min-time
  (4.4665, 0 sw)": the minimum-time solution. It is the left endpoint / anchor
  of the whole trade: zero coast arcs, maximum ΔV. Every min-fuel point must
  lie to its right (more time) and below it (less ΔV), and they all do.

## 4. The marker classes (colors)

Every stored solution is one circle. Its color is its certification tier,
assigned by `verify_tf_front.m` (details of the gates in Section 6):

| Color | Class | Meaning |
|---|---|---|

This is a rigorous **upper bound** on the true front at that t_f — the real optimum there is at or below this point — but we cannot claim it is a local extremal. Currently 3 such points (older scatter with genuine coast-arc sign inconsistencies in their duals). |
| **Light-green circle** (`[0.62 0.82 0.55]` fill) | 2 — interior-certified | ALL first-order PMP gates pass with the robust-β estimator (burn/coast switching-law signs ≥99% correct, per-switch scale consistency MAD ≤5%, primer alignment ≤0.2°, transversality ≤1e-3 relative) — **except** the very first switch of the trajectory, whose implied scale is anomalous (see Section 7). The anomaly is flagged, not hidden: these points are certified extremal on the interior of the trajectory, with one open question at the first switch. | **Dark-green circle** (`[0.20 0.65 0.25]` fill) | 3 — FULL direct-certified | Everything class 2 requires, AND the first switch's implied scale is within 10% of the common scale. Multiple circles can share one t_f (solutions from different seeds/branches:
energy-seeded `_en`, neighbor-chain `_nb`, down-chain `_dn`, legacy). All are
plotted; none are discarded.

## 5. The two envelope lines

This is the pair most likely to confuse, so precisely:

- **Solid green line — "certified envelope."** Through the *best certified*
  point (class ≥ 2, i.e. light or dark green) at each factor: at each t_f
  where at least one certified solution exists, take the minimum-ΔV certified
  one and connect them. This is the curve we are willing to *defend as
  first-order extremal*: "the min-fuel front, as certified by the direct
  method's own KKT duals."

- **Dashed grey line — "best-known feasible envelope."** Through the minimum
  ΔV over **ALL** points at each factor, certified or not. This is the honest
  statement "the true front lies at or below this line," because every point —
  even a grey one — is dynamically feasible and hence an upper bound.

**How to read the pair:** wherever the dashed line dips *below* the solid line,
an uncertified solution beats the best certified one at that t_f — that gap is
*information*, a flag saying a better local minimum exists there and the
certification (or the certified solution) needs work. **As of 2026-07-09 the
two lines coincide everywhere**: at every t_f the best-known solution is also a
certified one. That coincidence is the plot's headline result — there are no
known-better-but-unverified points hiding anywhere on the front.

Envelope grouping detail: factors are rounded to milli-factor resolution
(`round(1000*factor)/1000`) before grouping, because continuation carries
accumulated float error (1.6500000000000001 vs 1.65) that would otherwise
split one t_f into two groups and zig-zag the envelope.

## 6. How certification works (what the tiers actually test)

For each solution we have the NLP's own KKT duals for the dynamics constraints
(`lamDef`, 8×N) — these are the discrete costates. Pontryagin says the optimal
throttle is bang-bang in the switching function

    S = 1 − β·W,   W = ‖λ_v‖·c/m − λ_m

with a single positive scale β (which absorbs the trapezoid-weight / Sundman
time-dilation covector normalization). The check ("empirical-β"):

1. Find all throttle switches in the stored solution.
2. At each switch k, S must vanish, so the implied scale is β_k = 1/W_k.
   If the solution is extremal, all β_k agree.
3. **β = median of the β_k** (robust; an earlier least-squares estimator was
   dragged by one outlier switch and produced cascading false failures —
   diagnosed by `diag_beta_checker.m`).
4. Gates: sign of S correct on ≥99% of burn intervals AND ≥99% of coast
   intervals; robust spread of β_k (median absolute deviation) ≤5%; thrust
   direction anti-parallel to the primer vector λ_v within 0.2°;
   transversality |λ_m(t_f)| ≤ 1e-3 relative to max|λ_m|.
5. Tier: all gates pass AND |β_1/β − 1| ≤ 0.10 → **class 3 (FULL)**;
   all gates pass but the first switch is outside 10% → **class 2 (interior)**;
   any gate fails → **class 1 (feasible only)**.

## 7. The first-switch anomaly (why "interior" exists at all)

Across the upper band, every solution shows the same pattern: all switches
except the FIRST share a common scale to <0.8% MAD (essentially perfect), while
the first switch's implied scale decays smoothly with t_f — 1.00 at 1.12–1.15x,
0.93 at 1.20x, 0.84 at 1.25x, down to ~0.12–0.32 by 1.40–1.45x. It is a real,
systematic effect, not noise. Two candidate explanations, deliberately left
open:

- mesh under-resolution near the first switch (early trajectory = deep in the
  gravity well, fastest dynamics even after Sundman regularization), or
- genuine local non-extremality of the first burn/coast boundary.

Rather than either hiding this (certifying anyway) or over-reacting (failing
25 otherwise-perfect solutions), the two-tier gate quarantines exactly this
one question into the class-2/class-3 distinction. The planned arbiter is an
independent indirect solve (`ms_band` machinery) which carries exact
continuous costates and will say definitively whether the first switch is
where the direct solution puts it. Note 1.25x was **demoted** from the old
certified set by this analysis (first-switch scale 0.84) — an example of the
gate being honest rather than generous.

## 8. Shape of the front — what the data says

- ΔV falls steeply from the min-time anchor as t_f grows: extra time buys
  coast arcs through apogee where thrust is inefficient.
- **Campaign minimum: ΔV = 2.434 km/s at 1.65x (≈46 days)** — 45.5% below
  min-time.
- The curve is NOT monotone in the stored data: values at 1.70–1.85x sit
  *above* 2.434. Those points are **dominated**: by loiter monotonicity (any
  solution at t_f can be flown at t_f' > t_f by adding terminal coast — modulo
  a tulip-period phasing caveat), the true front at t_f ≥ 1.65x is ≤ 2.434.
  So the certified points at 1.70–1.85x are honest local minima of their
  family but are known not to be the front there. A planned probe re-attacks
  that tail seeded from the 1.65x minimum.
- Solution families connect over a fold at ~1.40–1.45x, where the switch
  count morphs (22 → 43 switches). Points on either side belong to different
  families; both families are represented in the stored set.
- The band 1.01x–1.11x (just above min-time) is absent: it is a hard
  bifurcation zone that resists all direct methods so far; a separate
  multiple-shooting attack (`ms_band`, other terminal) is in progress there.

## 9. What the plot does NOT claim

- **Not global optimality.** Green (either shade) = the solution satisfies
  first-order necessary conditions per its own duals. A better family could
  still exist at any t_f; the dashed line is only "best known."
- **No second-order/conjugate-point check** anywhere yet.
- **No independent-method confirmation** yet (class 4 empty).
- Class-2 points carry the explicit first-switch caveat of Section 7.
- The 1.70–1.85x certified points are certified *as extremals of their
  family*, while simultaneously known to be dominated (Section 8) — "certified"
  and "on the true front" are different claims, and the plot separates them.
